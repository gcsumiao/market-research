Market: The US digital multimeter market is an estimated ~$350M segment growing at 6-8% CAGR, driven by EV infrastructure, aging vehicle fleet (avg 12.8 yrs), and smart-home adoption. On Amazon, the automotive DMM subcategory generates $370K-$410K/month across 160-213 ASINs.

The 3320 drives 81% of units; the 3340 drives 32% of revenue. Innova's real strength is omnichannel—especially its AutoZone partnership, which Amazon data does not capture.

Audio DMM Concept: No major US brand offers a multimeter with spoken reading output. Screen readability is a top-3 Amazon review complaint. 5.1 Two-Channel Read

Amazon is a useful window into digital demand and consumer behavior: across the last 15 months Innova moved roughly $281K in revenue on 7,428 units (~$19K and ~495 units per month), with 3320 as the volume driver and 3340 as the margin driver at a blended $37.84 ASP. That is a stable, defensible cashflow line — but on its own it materially understates Innova's real US market position.

AutoZone tells a different, much larger story. Across the nine sampled weeks in the weekly_detail feed, 3320 averaged ~975 units / ~$34.9K per week and 3340 averaged ~122 units / ~$8.4K per week. On a rolling 52-week basis, 3320 is at ~53,078 units and ~$1.91M, and 3340 at ~4,960 units and ~$350K. In other words, a single week of AutoZone sell-through for 3320 is larger than a full month of Amazon revenue for the entire Innova DMM line.

The retail picture is also broader than the Amazon two-SKU frame suggests. 3306 — a relatively minor ASIN online — is a genuine retail driver at AutoZone: ~1,061 units / ~$25.4K per week on average and ~55,628 units / ~$1.34M L52. Innova's retail franchise is therefore a three-SKU story (3306, 3320, 3340), not a two-SKU story, and 3320's role as volume anchor is confirmed — not invented — by the Amazon data.

5.5 Strategic Implication

Implication for the refresh decision: Amazon is a useful but incomplete view of Innova's DMM performance. AutoZone demonstrates materially stronger retail scale and confirms that 3320 is a broad franchise anchor (not just an Amazon SKU), that 3340's margin role is real but volumetrically small, and that 3306 adds a third meaningful leg to the retail story. The core DMM line is worth defending and selectively refreshing — and that refresh decision should be sized against the combined Amazon + AutoZone evidence base, not against the Amazon P&L alone.

Decision — Core DMM business / refresh

GO (Scorecard A = 4.20 / 5.00). Refresh 3320 + 3340 listings (photography, A+ content, packaging copy, AutoZone end-cap story). This is a low-risk, high-fit move with known channels and a defensible retail moat. Treat it as a 2026 H1 cashflow-protection initiative, not a growth story.

Decision — Audio DMM / DMM Slider concept

CONDITIONAL — validate before you commit (Scorecard B = 3.00 / 5.00). The whitespace is genuine and the engineering is tractable, but demand is unproven and WTP is a guess. Do not fund a full SKU launch yet. Instead, run a $500–$2,000 validation sprint: (1) a landing-page smoke test with the key benefit, (2) 10–15 structured interviews with AutoZone DIY customers and technicians, (3) a price-ladder survey. Re-score B1, B2, B3 after the data comes in. If any two of those move to ≥4, escalate to full development.

Final portfolio recommendation

This is a portfolio call, not a single-product score. Innova has two distinct DMM opportunities sitting side-by-side:

  1. Defend & refresh the core (Quick Win quadrant) — ship this in H1 2026. It protects a ~$4.0M combined annual revenue base (Amazon + AutoZone), with AutoZone carrying the majority of that value. No new technology, no new category education.
  2. Validate the audio concept (Long-Shot-that-could-become-a-Big-Bet) — spend weeks, not quarters, proving or disproving demand before committing engineering. The value of this cell is the option, not the forecast.

Do both in parallel. They do not compete for the same budget line or the same team. The refresh is a marketing/listing initiative; the audio validation is a research/prototype initiative. Treating them as one program is what produced the previous blended-score problem in the first place.

What still needs data before further investment

  • Audio demand — landing-page conversion rate and interview sentiment.
  • Audio WTP — price-ladder survey output: is the premium $5, $15, or $30?
  • Refresh lift — before/after conversion delta on the 3320 listing once new creative is live.
  • Retail off-Amazon share — AutoZone 9-week TYLY weekly data now confirmed; O'Reilly + Advance sell-through still needed for full picture.
  • Acoustic feasibility in shop environments — a quick speaker-in-noise test, before any tooling spend.
