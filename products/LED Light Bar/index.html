Product Definition

MODU-LIGHT PRO Commercial — 5-in-1 LED Work Platform

One rechargeable aluminum light bar with two detachable 360° arms, 1/4"-20 accessory mounts, and rubber-coated spring-loaded clamps that grip plastic or metal. Five use zones from one purchase: under-hood · under-vehicle · interior · bench/diagnostic · hand-held.

Step 1 · Problem Research

Multi-Zone Lighting Is Unsolved for the Commercial Tech

Real & Sized

~52M US DIY auto consumers + ~900K professional techs. Forum activity across Garage Journal, BobIsTheOilGuy, JL Wrangler Forums confirms persistent demand.

Aluminum Hoods Expand It

Magnetic mounting — basis of 62.6% of Amazon units — fails on aluminum. F-150 (US #1 vehicle) is aluminum since 2015. EV & luxury follow.

Commercial Shops Carry 3+ Lights

Under-hood bar + underbody light + handheld flashlight + headlamp is standard kit. $300–$600 per bay in single-purpose lights.

Shadows = #1 Complaint

Single-point lights leave shadows behind components. Multiple light sources (not one brighter light) is the proven fix.

Step 3 · Consumer Voice

What Commercial & DIY Users Actually Say

🧲 Magnetic Failures

#1 frustration. Magnets weak on modern engine bays (plastic covers, aluminum hoods). Clamps that grip any surface solve this directly.

🔦 Shadows

Single-point sources cast deep shadows behind components. Users report carrying 2–3 lights; multi-source illumination is the top wish.

⏱️ Battery Life

2–3 hours on high is common. Longer repairs run out mid-job. Recurring complaint across SYLSTAR, MechaniLite, Ropelux.

🎯 Adjustability

Most lights: ~180°. Users want "point it anywhere and it stays" — full 360° single-knob is the desired spec.

🚗 Paint & Interior Risk

Bare metal hooks could stain cloth/leather interior surfaces; rubber-coated hooks are already standard on several competitors (NEIKO foam, Milwaukee M12, others). MODU-LIGHT must match this bar — not claim it as a first-of-kind feature. The real differentiation is the rubber spring clamp (grips any surface), not the hook.

🔧 No One-System Answer

Hood + under-vehicle + interior in one session needs multiple lights today. Users also substitute with headlamps (light tracks gaze, not work area) and flashlights (one hand busy). Each option fails on a different ergonomic dimension — reinforcing the multi-zone platform case.

🪝 Hooks Are Already Common

Many competitor lights already have magnets and hooks; some bars have expandable hooks. The unmet need is not "give me a hook" — it is "give me a mount that works on plastic shrouds, aluminum hoods, and trim panels without scratching or staining." That is a clamp problem.
