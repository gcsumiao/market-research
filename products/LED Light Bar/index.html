Product Definition

MODU-LIGHT PRO — Modular Under-Hood LED Work Light Bar

A modular, rechargeable LED work light bar for hands-free automotive repair. Covers three use zones — under-hood, vehicle interior, and under-vehicle — with a single system of detachable arms, threaded accessory mounts, and rubber-coated spring hooks.

Key Differentiator: First product to combine detachable arms + 1/4" threaded mounts + rubber spring hooks + 360° single-knob adjust in one system. A "lighting platform," not a single-purpose light bar.

Step 1 — Problem Research

Under-Hood Lighting: A Universal, Expanding Pain Point

The Problem is Real

~52M Americans do regular DIY car work. Engine bay shadows are a top-3 frustration for home mechanics. Forum analysis across Garage Journal, Bob Is The Oil Guy, and JL Wrangler Forums confirms persistent demand.2

Aluminum Hoods Are Expanding It

Magnetic mounting — the basis of 62%+ of work lights — fails on aluminum hoods. Ford F-150 (US #1 vehicle) has been aluminum since 2015. EV and luxury platforms follow. Growing pool of underserved users whose lights don't stick.

Multi-Zone Lighting Unmet

Mechanics need hood + under-vehicle + interior lighting in the same repair session. Today this requires 2–3 separate lights. No single product addresses all three zones.

Shadows Are the #1 Complaint

Single-point lights create deep shadows behind engine components. Users describe needing multiple lights, constant repositioning, and using headlamps as backups — all signs of an unsolved problem.2

Step 3 — Consumer Voice

What Real Users Say

🧲 Magnetic Failures

#1 frustration. Magnets too weak, lights fall off, don't work on aluminum hoods. Forum users note very little steel left in modern engine bays.2

🔦 Shadows

Single-point sources create shadows behind components. Users need 2–3 lights, constant repositioning. Multi-point illumination is the top wish.

⏱️ Battery Life

Many lights deliver only 2–3 hours on high. Users doing longer jobs run out mid-repair. Recurring complaint on SYLSTAR, MechaniLite, Ropelux.

🎯 Poor Adjustability

Most lights: 180° max. Users spend nearly as much time repositioning as turning fasteners. "Point it anywhere and it stays" is the universal desire.2

🚗 Hood Scratching

Metal hooks and bare magnets scratch paint, especially on newer vehicles. Rubber/foam padding mentioned repeatedly as must-have.

🔧 No Multi-Zone

Hood + under-vehicle + interior in one session requires carrying multiple lights. Universal frustration — no single product solves it.
