Step 1 of 5 — Problem Research

Problem Research

Independent auto repair shops own 5 separate analog gauges for the 5 core pressure tests. Each tool costs $25–$175, requires its own adapters, and none retain readings. The pro-tier solution that does exist — OTC 3492 — is $1,300–$1,800, beyond the reach of most of the 174,200 US shops.

Pain pattern: pro techs consolidate to fewer, more versatile tools as their toolbox matures; the current 5-gauge analog toolkit is friction-heavy, slow to document, and has no peak-hold or memory. Digital is the clear direction of travel.

Decision Gate

5-Test GO/NO-GO Verdict

Each test asks whether Innova can realistically win in a category that is fragmented, analog-dominated, and small in absolute dollars. The bar is not category demand — it is defensibility, revenue materiality, and risk-adjusted return.

TestVerdictJustification
1. Demand reality
Proof of buying?		 CONDITIONAL PASS No direct SKU velocity exists for a DIY 5-in-1 digital platform because the product does not exist today. Two-way triangulation is strong: AUTOOL PT610 proves DIYers buy digital single-function at ~$119[8] and OTC 3492 proves the 5-in-1 bundle sells at pro tier[9]. Amazon category revenue of $1.03M/month across 564 ASINs confirms active buying[1]. The DIY 5-in-1 slot itself is inferred, not observed — which is why this conditions rather than passes outright.
2. Defensible position
Win or only compete?		 CONDITIONAL PASS Defensibility is channel-specific and brand-specific. Product-side moat is thin: a 5-sensor platform can be copied inside 12–18 months by a well-funded competitor. The real moat is Innova's retail relationships (AutoZone, Walmart, Harbor Freight, O'Reilly) and its 40-year diagnostic-brand equity[12], neither of which AUTOOL, LINXINO, or an Amazon long-tail competitor can replicate inside 24–36 months. That moat is real in retail but absent on Amazon, which is why this conditions rather than fully passes.
3. Revenue worth the effort
Y3 vs. org capacity?		 CONDITIONAL PASS Base-case Y3 of $2.80M sits at the low edge of the mid-size SKU band and above the $1M floor. Upside of $4.50M clears the band comfortably but requires national retail. Risk case of $1.95M still clears minimum viability. The revenue justifies the SKU slot only if it pulls through Innova's existing retail channel — a pure-Amazon outcome lands the SKU in defensive-play territory.
4. Strategic fit
Leverages core?		 PASS The 5-in-1 sits squarely inside Innova's diagnostic-tool category, leverages the 5610 CarScan Pro brand permission[12], and uses existing supplier, distribution, and retail relationships. It is a natural line extension — the brand question has a clear affirmative answer.
5. Risk-adjusted ROI
After 50% haircut, profitable?		 CONDITIONAL PASS Applying the methodology's 50% revenue haircut to the base case yields risk-adjusted Y3 revenue of ~$1.40M. At supplier-direct economics ($80–95 landed, ~50% gross margin), contribution margin remains positive after ACoS, warranty, and returns. ROI clears Innova's cost of capital at the conservative case, but the margin of safety is modest and depends on retail pilot executing in H2.
Overall verdict: CONDITIONAL GO

One PASS (strategic fit) and four CONDITIONAL PASSES (demand, defensibility, revenue, risk-adjusted ROI). No test fails outright, which satisfies the decision rule for CONDITIONAL GO. The opportunity is real but tightly bounded — revenue sits at the low end of the SKU band, defensibility is channel-specific (retail, not Amazon), and demand is triangulated rather than directly observed. Proceed only if the pre-launch conditions in the final section clear; otherwise reverts to NO-GO.

Final verdict: CONDITIONAL GO

The US DIY pressure-test category is fragmented (564 Amazon ASINs across five sub-categories), analog-dominated (only 8.16% digital), and small in absolute dollars ($1.03M monthly on Amazon)[1]. The decision in five lines

QuestionAnswer
Is the category accessible to Innova?Yes. Known supply chain, existing retail relationships, no new certifications. Known category — a new SKU inside Innova's diagnostics line.
Is demand real?Conditionally. The exact 5-in-1 DIY SKU does not exist today, but AUTOOL PT610 and OTC 3492 triangulate both halves of the bet (digital + bundle) with real transactional evidence.

Pre-launch conditions for proceeding

  1. Written regional retail commitment. Shelf placement or PO intent from at least one of AutoZone, Walmart, Harbor Freight, or O'Reilly — with volume and price terms agreed. Retail is the structural moat; it must be real before launch, not after.
  2. Supplier-direct unit economics confirmed. Landed cost $80–$95 with ~50% gross margin on a signed supplier quote. MOQ and lead times must support Y1 plan.
  3. 250-unit Amazon soft launch validation. Fuel Pressure Tester keyword beach-head, Vine reviews gathered, A/B test $179 vs. $199, and 90-day gates: conversion ≥ 2.0%, return rate ≤ 6%. Fail below 1.2% or returns above 10% returns the decision to NO-GO.
  4. Retail as majority channel by Y2. Amazon-primary scaling exposes the plan to commodity pressure from unbranded DTC. Y2+ plan must show retail carrying ≥ 50% of unit volume.
