Phase 4

5-Test GO/NO-GO Verdict

Each test asks whether a new entrant can realistically win in the current competitive field — not simply whether the category sustains demand. In commodity-tilted categories the discriminating questions are defensibility, risk-adjusted return, and the ease with which an incumbent can replicate a new SKU's proposition. The tests below apply that bar.

TestVerdictJustification
1. Demand reality
Proof of buying?		 PASS Real transactional velocity on directly comparable products. VEVOR's 6.5L Dual-Mode moves ~1,383 u/mo at $43.99 and EWK's comparable SKU moves ~1,292 u/mo at $60.99; the Dual-Mode sub-segment clears ~$329K/month on Amazon[1]. The category is proven and has measured consumer willingness to pay in the target price band.
2. Defensible position
Win or only compete?		 CONDITIONAL PASS Product-side moat is thin — quart graduation is a silkscreen change and a transparent strip is a plastic-grade substitution, both copyable inside a single manufacturing cycle. Innova's defensibility rests on a different axis: established shelf placement and category-manager relationships at AutoZone, NAPA, O'Reilly, and Walmart[13][18][19] that VEVOR / EWK cannot replicate inside 24–36 months. That distribution moat is genuine and durable in the retail channel; it is not present on the Amazon shelf, which is where the category's online revenue concentrates. Defensibility exists but is channel-specific, which is why this test conditions rather than passes outright.
3. Revenue worth the effort
Y3 vs org capacity?		 CONDITIONAL PASS Base-case Y3 of $3.58M sits inside the mid-size SKU threshold and is supported by a directly comparable benchmark: VEVOR's Dual-Mode SKU clears ~$731K annually on Amazon alone. Combined retail-plus-Amazon distribution puts $3–4M within a defensible range, and the conservative case of $2.33M still clears the minimum-viability floor. The condition is that the unit-share assumption against 822 active brands must hold; the number is achievable but not comfortable.
4. Strategic fit
Leverages core?		 CONDITIONAL PASS The extractor sits in the right aisle and reaches the same DIY buyer as Innova's diagnostics line. It is, however, a consumable-adjacent service tool rather than an instrumentation product, which weakens brand-permission carry. Fit is real but thinner than it looks, and it does not on its own neutralize the category's commodity dynamics.
5. Risk-adjusted ROI
After 50% haircut, profitable?		 CONDITIONAL PASS Applying the skill's 50% revenue haircut to the base case yields risk-adjusted Y3 revenue of ~$1.79M. At supplier-direct wholesale economics ($24–27 ASP, ~55% gross margin), contribution margin remains positive after realistic ACoS, warranty, and return costs. The return clears Innova's cost of capital at the conservative case, though the margin of safety is modest and depends on the pre-launch validation steps executing successfully.
Overall verdict: CONDITIONAL GO

One PASS (demand reality) and four CONDITIONAL PASSES (defensible position, revenue, strategic fit, risk-adjusted ROI). No test fails outright, which satisfies the decision rule for a CONDITIONAL GO. The opportunity is real but tightly bounded — defensibility is channel-specific (retail, not Amazon), revenue sits at the low edge of the mid-size band, and the risk-adjusted return is positive but modest. Proceed only if the pre-launch conditions in the final verdict section are met; otherwise the opportunity reverts to a NO-GO.

Pre-launch conditions for proceeding

The CONDITIONAL GO is contingent on the following validation steps completing before commitment to full launch. Any of these failing returns the decision to a NO-GO:

  1. Written retail-channel commitment. Shelf placement or PO intent from at least one of AutoZone, NAPA, O'Reilly, or Walmart, with volume and price terms agreed. Retail distribution is the structural advantage this case rests on; it has to be made real before launch, not after.
  2. Supplier-direct unit economics confirmed. Wholesale ASP of $24–27 with ~55% gross margin verified on a signed supplier quote, with MOQ and lead-time terms that support the Y1 launch plan. The risk-adjusted ROI test rests on this cost position.
  3. 250-unit Amazon soft-launch validation. Vine reviews gathered, A/B test on $54.99 vs $49.99 price point completed, review velocity tracked against the ~$329K/month Dual-Mode sub-segment benchmark. The test must confirm unit-share assumptions before full-scale commitment.
  4. Channel-mix plan with retail as a majority contributor by Year 2. Amazon-primary scaling exposes the plan to the review moat and price compression that dominate the online channel. The path to Y3 base revenue requires retail to carry meaningful volume; the launch plan needs to show how.

If all four conditions are met, proceed to launch. If any fail, the verdict reverts to NO-GO and the SKU slot is redeployed.
