Step 1 of 5 — Problem Research

Is the EVAP leak-detection pain real?

Yes. Smoke machines solve a real diagnostic problem for EVAP leaks, vacuum leaks, intake leaks, and exhaust leaks. The pain is not awareness; it is safe use. Forum and Q&A evidence repeatedly flags that EVAP systems must be tested at very low pressure and can be damaged by generic high-pressure smoke tools.

Step 1 Verdict: Real Problem, Safety Gap

The category has a clear problem to solve, but Innova's product must lead with EVAP-safe pressure control rather than generic smoke output.

Step 3 of 5 — Consumer Voice

Users want safe pressure, less guesswork, and complete kits

Consumer and forum evidence points to a practical workflow gap: users can read EVAP codes but struggle to find leaks safely. The strongest messages are safety, complete adapters, clear instructions, and confidence that the tool will not damage the EVAP system.

Pain PointWhat Customers NeedReport Action

Incomplete adaptersEVAP service port, cone, intake bladder, useful hose lengthKeep adapters in the base kit, not upsells.
Unclear setupShort leak-type videos and code-to-smoke workflowUse QR onboarding and Innova diagnostic language.
Cheap-tool reliability concernsWarranty, better clamps, thermal protection, visible fluid levelBundle SKU 1 as retail-grade, not marketplace generic.
Step 3 Verdict: EVAP Safety is the Message

The winning consumer promise is simple: read the code, find the leak, and avoid EVAP damage.
